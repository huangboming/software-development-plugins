# Interview Prep

Produces a prep document for a semi-structured interview: discovery interviews (problem exploration) or evaluative interviews (reaction to an existing experience).

## Prep Document Template

```markdown
# Interview Prep: [Topic]

**Date:** [today]
**Method:** Semi-structured interview ([discovery | evaluative])
**Estimated sessions:** [5–8 recommended]
**Session length:** [45–60 min]

## Research Goals

1. [What we want to learn — framed as questions, not hypotheses]
2. [...]
3. [...]

**Grounding signals:** [List any signals from .product/discover/signals/ that motivated this research, with file links]

## Recruitment Criteria

**Include:**
- [Behavioral criterion — what they DO, not demographics]
- [...]

**Exclude:**
- [Who to screen out and why]
- [...]

**Sample target:** [number] participants across [segments if applicable]

## Screener

[5–8 questions to qualify participants. Mix of closed (filter) and open (verify). Include disqualifying answers.]

1. [Closed question] → Disqualify if [answer]
2. [...]

## Discussion Guide

### Warm-up (5 min)
- [Rapport-building question — about them, not the topic]
- [Transition question bridging to the topic]

### Core Topics (35–45 min)

**Topic 1: [name]** (~[X] min)
- [Primary question — open-ended]
  - Probe: [follow-up if they stay surface-level]
  - Probe: [follow-up to get a specific example]

**Topic 2: [name]** (~[X] min)
- [...]

**Topic 3: [name]** (~[X] min)
- [...]

### Closing (5 min)
- "Is there anything about [topic] that I didn't ask about but should have?"
- "Who else should I talk to about this?"
- [Thank and explain next steps]

**Participant:** [ID, not name]

- [...]

3. [...]

- [...]
```

## Gotchas

- **Write questions that start with "how" or "tell me about," not "do you" or "would you."** Yes/no questions kill depth. "Do you find onboarding confusing?" yields "yes." "Walk me through what happened when you first signed up" yields a story with actionable detail.
- **Never embed the product's terminology in questions.** "How do you use our Smart Filters?" presumes awareness and frames the user's experience through the product's lens. Ask about the activity: "How do you narrow down results when you're searching?"
- **Order topics from broad to narrow, not the reverse.** Starting with specific features anchors the conversation. Start with the user's world, then zoom in.
- **Include 2 probes per core question.** Without probes, interviewers accept the first answer. Probes like "Can you give me a specific example?" and "What happened next?" are where the real insights live.
- **Cap core topics at 3–4.** More topics means less depth on each. Cut ruthlessly — if a topic is "nice to have," it doesn't belong.
- **Screener must filter on behavior, not self-assessment.** "Are you tech-savvy?" is useless. "How many SaaS tools do you use weekly for work?" filters on observable behavior.
