# Diary Study Prep

Produces a prep document for a diary study — a longitudinal method where participants self-report experiences in context over days or weeks. Best for capturing behaviors that unfold over time, are hard to recall retrospectively, or are influenced by context.

## Contents

1. [Prep Document Template](#prep-document-template) — Full template with entry prompts, participant instructions, check-in schedule, and closing interview guide
2. [Gotchas](#gotchas) — Entry prompt brevity, trigger precision, attrition planning

## Prep Document Template

```markdown
# Diary Study Prep: [Topic]

**Date:** [today]
**Method:** Diary study
**Duration:** [1–4 weeks]
**Estimated participants:** [8–15, recruiting 20–25% extra for attrition]
**Entry frequency:** [event-triggered | daily | specific intervals]

## Research Goals

1. [What longitudinal patterns or in-context behaviors we want to capture]
2. [...]

**Grounding signals:** [List any signals from .product/discover/signals/ that motivated this study]

## Recruitment Criteria

**Include:**
- [People who regularly encounter the target situation]
- [Must be willing to commit to [duration] of self-reporting]

**Exclude:**
- [...]

**Attrition plan:** Recruit [N + 20–25%] to account for dropouts. Plan a mid-study check-in to re-engage.

## Entry Prompts

Participants complete an entry each time [trigger event occurs | at end of day | at specified intervals].

### Required Fields (every entry)

1. **When:** Date and time
2. **What happened:** [1–2 sentence open-text prompt — keep specific to the behavior of interest]
   - Example prompt: "Describe what you were trying to do and what happened."
3. **Context:** [Where were you? What device? Who else was involved?]
4. **Feeling:** [Single-select emotion scale or emoji scale — keep fast to answer]

### Optional Fields (rotate or include selectively)

- [Specific follow-up relevant to research goals — e.g., "What did you do next?"]
- [Photo/screenshot prompt if relevant — "Take a photo of what you see"]
- [Rating scale for a specific dimension — e.g., "How confident were you? 1–5"]

## Participant Instructions

[Provide to participants at onboarding. Keep under 1 page.]

```
Thank you for participating in this study!

**What we're asking:** Over the next [duration], please fill out a short entry each time [trigger event]. Each entry takes about 2 minutes.

**When to log an entry:** [Describe the trigger clearly — "any time you..." / "at the end of each day"]

**How to submit:** [Tool/channel — Google Form, Slack, text message, app]

**Tips:**
- Log entries as close to the moment as possible — details fade fast
- There are no right or wrong answers — we want your honest experience
- If you miss an entry, don't backfill from memory — just continue with the next one

**Questions?** Contact [researcher contact info]
```

## Check-in Schedule

| Timing | Action |
|---|---|
| Day 1 | Onboarding call — walk through instructions, do a practice entry together |
| Day [3–4] | First check-in — review entries so far, clarify any confusion |
| Mid-study | Engagement check — thank participants, address any issues |
| End of study | Closing interview — debrief, explore patterns from their entries |

## Closing Interview Guide

[30-min semi-structured interview after the diary period ends. Use diary entries as discussion stimuli.]

- "Looking back, which entry stands out to you as most memorable? Why?"
- "Did your experience change over the [duration]? How?"
- "Were there times you wanted to log an entry but didn't? What happened?"
- [2–3 questions specific to patterns observed in their entries]

## Analysis Framework

| Analysis type | What to look for |
|---|---|
| Frequency patterns | Which triggers occur most? When? |
| Emotional patterns | How do feelings correlate with context or trigger type? |
| Behavioral patterns | What do participants do before/after the trigger? |
| Longitudinal change | Does behavior or attitude shift over the study period? |
| Segment differences | Do different participant types show different patterns? |
```

## Gotchas

- **Entry prompts must be completable in under 2 minutes.** Every minute beyond that increases abandonment rate. If you need depth, save it for the closing interview — the diary captures breadth and frequency.
- **Never ask participants to backfill missed entries from memory.** Retrospective recall is exactly what diary studies avoid. If they miss an entry, it's gone. Design the prompt frequency and channel to minimize misses instead.
- **Define the trigger event precisely.** "Log an entry when something interesting happens" yields nothing. "Log an entry each time you switch between two apps to complete a task" yields data.
- **Recruit 20–25% over target.** Attrition in diary studies is 15–30%. Plan for it at recruitment, not mid-study.
- **Use entry data as interview stimuli.** The closing interview is where insights crystallize. Without it, you have data points but not understanding. Always include a closing interview in the prep.
